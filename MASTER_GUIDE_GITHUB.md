## 🎤 INTERVIEW TALKING POINTS

### When They Ask: "Walk us through your project"

**Opening (1 min):**
> "I built a market surveillance alert system for Nord Pool that monitors day-ahead prices across 13 Nordic and Baltic zones. The system uses 5 complementary detection methods - statistical outliers, negative prices, extreme thresholds, cross-border spreads, and pattern breaks - to identify suspicious behavior."

**Technical (2 min):**
> "I implemented this in Python using pandas for data processing, scipy for statistical analysis, and matplotlib for visualization. The Z-score method flags prices deviating more than 3 standard deviations from a 9-month baseline. The spread anomaly detection proved most valuable - 68% of alerts came from this method, immediately signaling congestion as the core issue."

**Results (2 min):**
> "The system generated 1,244 alerts during the October 6-12 monitoring week, with 373 alerts concentrated on October 7 when Lithuania and Latvia hit 1,173 EUR/MWh. Through systematic investigation, I discovered this coincided with Storm Amy, which created high wind generation in the north (negative prices) while transmission constraints prevented power flow to the Baltic states (extreme prices)."

**Investigation (2 min):**
> "I cross-referenced with Nord Pool's UMM publications and found critical context: the day-ahead coupling system had required manual fallback on September 25, suggesting pre-existing stress. On October 7, results publication was delayed by an hour, and there was intraday partial decoupling. This confirmed the extreme prices resulted from legitimate physical constraints under storm conditions, not market manipulation."

**Professional Judgment (1 min):**
> "This taught me that surveillance requires balancing algorithmic detection with contextual analysis. High prices don't automatically mean manipulation - they can reflect real scarcity. My role is to distinguish between legitimate market response and artificial manipulation, which requires understanding both the technical indicators and the physical market fundamentals."
